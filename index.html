How does this work?

1. Choose a Random Race

Every character belongs to a race, one of the many intelligent humanoid species in the D&D world. The most common player character races are Dwarves, Elves, Halflings, and Humans (however, this generator uses all possible races). Some races also have subraces, such as Mountain-Dwarf or Wood-Elf.1

2. Choose a Random Class

Every adventurer is a member of a class. Class broadly describes a characterâ€™s vocation, what special talents he or she possesses, and the tactics he or she is most likely to employ when exploring a dungeon, fighting monsters, or engaging in a tense negotiation.2

3. Roll Random Ability Scores

There are six abilities - Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma - and your character receives a score for each, which determines his or her ability to perform various actions.

Your character's six ability scores are generated randomly. Four 6-sided "dice" are rolled and the total of the highest three numbers are recorded. This is done five more times creating 6 random scores, one for each ability.

The random scores are ordered highest to lowest, and, because the importance of abilities depends on which class was generated, the abilities are sorted by most important, then randomized non-essential abilties, then least important ability. For example, if you roll a Barbarian, your most important ability is Strength; Dexterity, Constituation and Charisma are non-essential; and Wisdom is the least important.

4. Add Racial Modifiers to Ability Scores

Each race receives bonuses to certain abilities. After the abilities and scores have been sorted, your scores are modified based on which bonuses your race receives.

5. Calculate Modifiers

Once everything has been calculated, ability modifiers are determined by subtracting 10 from each score and then dividing the result by 2 (rounding down).

Want to see more details on each roll? Open up the console and enter showDetails = true. Then, reroll!
